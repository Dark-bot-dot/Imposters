About Our Team

We are a passionate group of students dedicated to solving real-world problems through human-centered design and technology.

Our Mission

Designing with Empathy

Our journey began with a simple question: How can we use technology to protect the most vulnerable members of our society?

We chose to focus on elderly care because falls among seniors represent one of the most preventable yet devastating health crises. Through extensive research, interviews with caregivers, and deep empathy mapping, we identified that the current solutions are either too expensive, too intrusive, or simply don't work when they're needed most.

Our mission is to create an affordable, reliable, and dignified safety solution that gives elderly individuals their independence back while giving their families peace of mind.

🎯

Our Vision

A world where no elderly person has to lie on the floor for hours after a fall, waiting for help that may never come on time.

The Crisis

Why Elderly Falls?

Understanding the magnitude and severity of the problem that motivated our project.

🛀

Bathroom Dangers

Bathrooms are the most common place for elderly falls due to wet, slippery floors. Without grab bars or non-slip mats, a simple shower can become life-threatening.

🏠

Living Alone

Many elderly individuals live independently. After a fall, they may be unable to reach a phone or move, leaving them stranded for hours with serious injuries.

💔

Psychological Impact

The fear of falling again causes anxiety, reduces confidence, and limits mobility — ironically increasing the risk of future falls due to inactivity.

💰

Healthcare Burden

Falls cost billions in healthcare annually. Hip fractures, head injuries, and prolonged hospital stays create massive emotional and financial stress on families.

After my mother fell in the bathroom and lay there for 3 hours before we found her, I knew something had to change. Current emergency buttons don't work when you can't reach them.

— Caregiver Interview, User Research Phase

Guiding Principles

Human-Centered Design

Every decision we made was rooted in empathy for the elderly and their caregivers. We didn't just build a device — we designed an experience that respects dignity while ensuring safety.
