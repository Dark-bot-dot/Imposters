Structured Brainwriting

How we used the 6-3-5 method to generate, organize, and refine over 100 ideas in just 30 minutes.

The Method

What is 6-3-5 Brainwriting?

The 6-3-5 Method is a structured brainstorming technique where 6 participants write down 3 ideas in 5 minutes, then pass their sheet to the next person who builds upon those ideas.

This method is incredibly powerful because it:

  • ⚡

    Eliminates Dominance

    Quiet team members get equal voice — no one dominates the discussion.

  • 🧠

    Builds on Ideas

    Each participant adds to previous ideas, creating hybrid solutions.

  • 📈

    High Volume Output

    In just 30 minutes, we generated 108 raw ideas to work with.

  • 🎯

    Structured & Fast

    No endless debates. Time pressure forces creative thinking.

How We Did It

Our Process

A step-by-step breakdown of how we applied the 6-3-5 method to our elderly care challenge.

1

Define Problem

We clearly framed our challenge: "How might we prevent or quickly respond to falls among elderly individuals living alone?"

2

Set Up Sheets

Each of 6 team members got a sheet divided into 3 rows (ideas) and 6 columns (rounds) with the problem statement at the top.

3

Round 1 (5 min)

Everyone silently wrote 3 original ideas for solving the elderly fall problem. No talking, no judgment.

4

Pass & Build

Sheets were passed clockwise. Each person read the previous ideas and added 3 new ones — building, combining, or improving.

5

6 Rounds Total

After 6 rounds (30 minutes total), every sheet had 18 ideas — 108 ideas across the entire team.

6

Cluster & Vote

We grouped similar ideas into themes: Wearables, Home Sensors, AI Systems, Physical Aids, and Emergency Response.

Ideas Generated

108 Ideas in 30 Minutes

⌚

Wearable Devices

Smart watches, wristbands, pendants, and ankle bands with fall detection sensors, GPS tracking, and emergency buttons.

24 ideas
🏠

Home Sensors

18 ideas
🤖

AI & Smart Systems

AI home assistants, voice-activated emergency systems, predictive fall algorithms, and behavior monitoring.

21 ideas
🦯

Physical Aids

Smart walking sticks, sensor-equipped grab bars, anti-slip footwear, balance training devices, and stair lifts.

15 ideas
📱

Emergency Response

Automatic SMS alerts, caregiver apps, neighbor notification systems, and integration with hospital networks.

16 ideas
💊

Health Monitoring

Medication reminders, hydration alerts, vital sign tracking, and integration with health records.

14 ideas

The 6-3-5 method forced us to think beyond our first obvious solution. By round 4, we were combining ideas we'd never have thought of alone — like a watch that detects falls AND monitors medication compliance.
