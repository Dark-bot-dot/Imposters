How we narrowed from multiple problems to one critical challenge, then explored various solutions to reach our final concept.

Our Journey

The Narrowing Funnel

A visual representation of how we systematically focused our efforts from a wide problem space to a single, actionable solution.

🔍 Phase 1: Broad Research

We started with a wide lens — elderly care in general. Health issues, mobility challenges, memory decline, social isolation, and safety concerns.

⬇️

📋 Phase 2: Two Problem Areas

Through empathy mapping and user interviews, we identified two major clusters: Falls & Physical Injury and Memory & Daily Tasks.

⬇️

🎯 Phase 3: One Focused Problem

We chose Falls & Physical Injury because it was the most urgent, life-threatening, and underserved by existing affordable solutions.

⬇️

💡 Phase 4: Multiple Solutions

Using the 6-3-5 method, we brainstormed 108 ideas across 6 categories: Wearables, Home Sensors, AI Systems, Physical Aids, Emergency Response, and Health Monitoring.

⬇️

📊 Phase 5: Top 5 Shortlisted

We evaluated all ideas using Feasibility, Desirability, and Viability criteria. The top 5 were: Smart Watch, Floor Mat, Grab Bars, AI Home Hub, and Walking Stick.

⬇️

🏆 Phase 6: Final Solution

The SafeGuard Smart Fall Detection Watch — wearable, reliable, and dignified.

Phase 1-2

From General Elderly Care to Two Critical Areas

Initial Problem Space

When we began our research, we looked at elderly care holistically. We identified numerous challenges faced by seniors living alone:

  • 🚶

    Mobility Issues

    Difficulty walking, climbing stairs, and maintaining balance.

  • 🧠

    Memory Decline

    Forgetting medications, meals, hygiene routines, and appointments.

  • 💔

    Social Isolation

    Loneliness, depression, and lack of regular human contact.

  • 🛡

    Safety & Falls

    High risk of falling with no one to help, leading to serious injuries.

  • 💰

    Financial Strain

    Medical bills, caregiver costs, and limited pension income.

💡

How We Narrowed to Two Areas

Through empathy mapping and caregiver interviews, two problems emerged as the most emotionally urgent and frequently mentioned: Falls (immediate physical danger) and Memory (gradual health decline). We documented these in our User Understanding page.

Phase 3

Choosing Our Focus: Falls & Injury

Why Falls Won

We had to choose ONE problem to solve within our student constraints. Here's why Falls & Physical Injury became our focus:

  • ⚡

    Immediate Life Threat

    A fall can cause death within hours if help doesn't arrive. Memory issues are serious but not immediately fatal.

  • 💪

    Technically Feasible

    Fall detection uses motion sensors we could learn and build. AI memory assistants require complex NLP beyond our scope.

  • 🌟

    High Impact

    Saving even one life from a timely alert justifies the entire project. The impact is immeasurable.

📋

Our Final Problem Statement

"Elderly individuals often experience reduced balance, muscle strength, and slower reflexes, making them highly prone to falls inside the house — especially in bathrooms, kitchens, and near beds. If they live alone, a fall can leave them unable to get up or call for help, leading to serious injuries or worse."

Phase 4-5

Exploring Multiple Solutions

With our problem defined, we used the 6-3-5 brainwriting method to generate 108 ideas, then shortlisted the top 5 concepts for detailed evaluation.

⌚ Smart Fall Detection Watch

Wrist-worn device with MPU6050 sensor, GSM alerts, and 10-second cancel window. Works everywhere the user goes.

★ FINAL SOLUTION

🛏️ Smart Fall Detection Mat

Pressure-sensitive mat placed near bed or bathroom. Detects sudden impact force and triggers alerts.

🥈 Runner-up

🦯 Smart Walking Stick

Sensor-equipped cane that detects abnormal tilt and movement. Alerts when a fall occurs during walking.

Rejected

🚽 Sensor-Based Grab Bars

Bathroom support bars with force sensors. Detects sudden grip force during a slip and triggers emergency alerts.

Rejected

🤖 AI Home Companion Device

Smart speaker with voice reminders, health monitoring, and emergency calling. Central hub for elderly care.

Rejected
Phase 6

Why the Watch Won

The SafeGuard Advantage

After evaluating all 5 concepts against our criteria (Feasibility 30%, Desirability 40%, Viability 30%), the Smart Fall Detection Watch scored 9.2/10 — the highest of all concepts.

  • 🌎

    Universal Coverage

    Works everywhere — bathroom, stairs, outdoors. Not limited to one room like mats or grab bars.

  • 👍

    No Stigma

    Looks like a normal watch. Elderly already comfortable wearing watches daily.

  • ⚡

    Instant Alerts

    GSM module sends SMS within seconds. No dependency on smartphone or WiFi.

  • 🎓

    Student-Feasible

    Uses Arduino, MPU6050, and SIM800L — all components we can learn and build.

🏆

The Decision

We unanimously chose the Wearable Smart Fall Detection Watch because it uniquely combines portability, affordability, and reliability — solving the exact problem we defined while respecting the dignity of elderly users.
